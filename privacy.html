Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the Strava Dashboard web application (the “App”, “we”, “us”) handles information when you use it. The App is an independent, personal-use analytics dashboard for your own Strava data. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Strava, Inc. By using the App you agree to this Policy.

1. Who we are

The App is operated by the individual or entity that deployed it (the “Operator”). For privacy questions, contact: opulunceenterprise@gmail.com.

2. Information we access

When you connect your Strava account, you authorize the App (via Strava’s OAuth) to access data associated with your account, which may include:

  • Profile information (name, profile photo, athlete ID, bikes/gear, all-time totals);
  • Activities and their details (distance, time, speed, elevation, heart rate, power, cadence, calories/energy, GPS routes/streams, photos, names, dates, locations);
  • Starred segments, segment efforts, and achievements/KOMs.

The OAuth scopes requested are read, activity:read_all, profile:read_all, and activity:write. The activity:write scope is used only when you explicitly choose to reassign gear on selected activities; the App does not otherwise modify your Strava data.

3. How we use information

  • To display your statistics, charts, maps, calendars, segments, and shareable “story” images within your browser;
  • To cache your activity data so the App loads faster and avoids repeatedly re-fetching from Strava;
  • To perform actions you explicitly request (e.g., reassigning gear).

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data for advertising, and we do not use it for any purpose beyond operating the features you use.

4. Where data is stored

  • Your browser — access/refresh tokens, your selected preferences (e.g., units, sport mode, last page), and a copy of your recent activities are stored in localStorage on your device.
  • Cache database — a copy of your recent activities may be stored, keyed to your Strava athlete ID, in a hosted database (Supabase) to speed up repeat visits.

We never receive or store your Strava password.

5. Third-party services (sub-processors)

The App relies on third parties that process data on our behalf or deliver the App. Each has its own privacy policy:

  • Strava, Inc. — source of your activity data (Strava Privacy Policy / API Agreement);
  • Supabase — caching database and serverless functions;
  • Vercel — website hosting;
  • Map tile & library providers (e.g., CARTO, OpenStreetMap, and content delivery networks) — for maps and assets.

6. Cookies & tracking

The App does not use advertising cookies or third-party analytics/tracking. It uses local browser storage (described above) solely to make the App function.

7. Data retention & deletion

  • Cached activity data refreshes periodically and is overwritten on refresh;
  • You can clear your tokens and this device’s cached data at any time by clicking Disconnect, or by clearing your browser storage;
  • You can revoke the App’s access entirely from your Strava → Settings → My Apps page;
  • To request deletion of any data held in the cache database, contact the email above.

8. Security

We take reasonable measures to protect data, but no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. You use the App at your own risk (see our Terms of Service).

9. Children

The App is not directed to children under the age required to hold a Strava account in your jurisdiction, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

10. International use

Your data may be processed in countries other than your own. By using the App you consent to such processing.

11. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated Policy.
